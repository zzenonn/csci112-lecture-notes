## Guide to Oral Exams

1. **Signup:** Oral examination signups will be posted on Canvas on October 7, 2025
2. **Exam Date:** All oral examinations will be conducted on October 11, 2025
3. **Problem Assignment:** Students will be randomly assigned a problem set on the day of the oral exam
4. **Preparation Requirement:** Students are expected to be prepared to answer any and all problem sets
5. **Additional Topics:** Questions about general MongoDB knowledge may be asked, including:
   - Sharding and replication
   - Data modeling concepts
   - Keys and indexing
   - All concepts covered in the course
6. **Time Allocation:** Each student will have 15 minutes total:
   - 10 minutes: Model discussion and demonstration
   - 5 minutes: Q&A session
7. **Guide Questions:** Do not be limited to the guide questions provided in each problem set
8. **Question Format:** Guide questions won't be asked one by one. They are meant to *guide* your understanding of the problem
9. **Coverage:** You don't need to answer all the guide questions. What is important is a strong understanding of the access pattern, implementation, and data model as noted in the rubric below.

## Logistics

1. **Preparation:** Review ALL problem sets thoroughly as you may be assigned any one randomly
2. **Technical Setup:** Ensure you have MongoDB access and can demonstrate implementations live. **If needed, you may prepare quietly on a classroom machine while the student preceeding you is completing their orals.**
3. **Materials:** You may bring notes but should be prepared to explain concepts without heavy reliance on written materials
4. **Focus:** Emphasis will be placed on understanding concepts and demonstrating working implementations
5. **Scope:** Be prepared for questions on any MongoDB topic covered in the course

## Grading Rubric

Each problem set will be evaluated using the following criteria. Each criterion is scored on a 0-4 point scale. The final grade will be calculated as the average of all criterion scores.

| Criterion | 4 pts - Excellent | 3 pts - Good | 2 pts - Fair | 1 pt - Poor | 0 pts - No Marks |
|-----------|-------------------|--------------|---------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Organization** | The response is overtly organized. The student helps the examiner understand the sequence and relationships of ideas by using organizational aids such as announcing the topic, previewing the organization, using transitions, and summarizing. | The response is organized. The examiner has no difficulty understanding the sequence and relationships among the ideas in the response. The ideas in the response can be outlined easily. | The organization of the response is mixed up and random. The examiner must make some assumptions about the sequence and relationship of ideas. | The response is so disorganized you cannot understand most of the response. | You cannot understand the response at all. |
| **Technical Implementation** | The student successfully demonstrates their data model by inserting sample data into MongoDB and executing all required query operations without errors. All aggregation pipelines (if required) work correctly and produce expected results. | The student demonstrates their data model by inserting sample data into MongoDB and executing most query operations successfully. Aggregation pipelines work but may have minor issues that are quickly resolved. | The student demonstrates basic implementation by inserting some data and executing simple queries. May struggle with complex queries or aggregation pipelines but shows understanding of the concepts. | The student shows limited ability to implement their data model. Can insert basic data but struggles with query operations or aggregation pipelines. | No working implementation demonstrated or complete inability to execute basic MongoDB operations. |
| **Data Model Design** | The student demonstrates comprehensive understanding of the specific data modeling pattern being studied. Clearly explains pattern characteristics, appropriate use cases, and design rationale. Shows mastery of pattern-specific concepts and terminology. | The student demonstrates good understanding of the data modeling pattern. Explains most pattern characteristics and use cases with minor gaps in reasoning or terminology. | The student demonstrates basic understanding of the data modeling pattern. Can identify some key characteristics but may struggle with explaining appropriate use cases or design rationale. | The student demonstrates limited understanding of the data modeling pattern. Shows minimal grasp of pattern concepts or appropriate applications. | No understanding of the data modeling pattern demonstrated or complete inability to explain pattern concepts. |
| **Follow-up Questions & General Knowledge** | The student demonstrates excellent ability to answer follow-up questions about their solution and shows comprehensive understanding of general MongoDB and NoSQL concepts. Can discuss sharding, replication, indexing, ACID properties, CAP theorem, and other database concepts with confidence and accuracy. | The student demonstrates good ability to answer most follow-up questions and shows solid understanding of general MongoDB and NoSQL concepts. Can discuss most database concepts with minor gaps in knowledge. | The student demonstrates basic ability to answer simple follow-up questions and shows limited understanding of general MongoDB and NoSQL concepts. May struggle with more advanced database topics. | The student demonstrates limited ability to answer follow-up questions and shows minimal understanding of general MongoDB and NoSQL concepts. Shows confusion about basic database principles. | No ability to answer follow-up questions or complete lack of understanding of MongoDB and NoSQL concepts. |
